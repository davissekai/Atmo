Why is the Harmattan season getting worse in Northern Ghana?

A
Atmo

The Harmattan season has intensified due to several interconnected factors:

Desertification: The Sahara is expanding southward at approximately 48 km per year, bringing more dust particles and drier air masses into the region.

Deforestation: Loss of forest cover in the Sahel region reduces natural windbreaks and moisture retention, allowing Harmattan winds to carry more dust over longer distances.

Temperature Changes: Rising global temperatures create stronger pressure differentials between the Sahara and the Gulf of Guinea, intensifying the dry winds.
